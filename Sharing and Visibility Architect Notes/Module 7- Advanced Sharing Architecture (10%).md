# 2. Implicit Sharing

Implicit Sharing is **automatic sharing generated by Salesforce**.

Developers cannot configure it directly.

It exists to maintain logical access between related records.

---

## Characteristics

- Automatically generated
- Cannot be manually edited
- Cannot be deleted
- Managed entirely by Salesforce

---

# Types of Implicit Sharing

Salesforce supports

- Parent Implicit Sharing
- Child Implicit Sharing

---

# LDV Best Practices

- Keep OWD as simple as possible.
- Prefer declarative sharing.
- Keep role hierarchy shallow.
- Minimize Public Group nesting.
- Avoid unnecessary Apex sharing.
- Reduce ownership changes.
- Use Deferred Sharing during migrations.
- Use Public Groups instead of sharing with individual users.

# Architect Best Practices

- Start with **Private OWD** and grant access incrementally.
- Understand and leverage **Implicit Sharing** before creating additional sharing rules.
- Prefer **Role Hierarchy** for management visibility and **Enterprise Territory Management** for geographic or market-based access.
- Use **Public Groups** instead of creating multiple duplicate sharing rules.
- Avoid excessive nesting of groups and overly deep role hierarchies.
- Enable **Deferred Sharing Calculation** before large administrative changes in production.
- Design sharing with **Large Data Volumes (LDV)** in mind to minimize recalculation costs.
- Use declarative sharing whenever possible and reserve Apex sharing for complex requirements.
